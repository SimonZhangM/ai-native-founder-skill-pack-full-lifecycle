# Validation and Research Reference

## Convert observations into testable hypotheses

Weak observation:

```text
People struggle with expense reporting.
```

Testable hypothesis:

```text
Finance managers at mid-market companies spend 4+ hours per week reconciling submissions because their current tools do not integrate with their accounting software.
```

A usable hypothesis includes:

- Persona or buyer.
- Problem context.
- Frequency.
- Severity.
- Current workaround.
- Why existing alternatives fail.
- What a solution must do.

## Disconfirming evidence

For every idea, ask:

1. What evidence would make this idea false?
2. Which competitors or substitutes are already solving it well enough?
3. Why might users say they want it but not adopt it?
4. Why might the buyer and user be different?
5. What regulation, workflow inertia, procurement requirement, or integration burden could block adoption?
6. What would a skeptical investor, buyer, or incumbent say?

## Competitive landscape tiers

- Direct competitors: solve the same job for the same user.
- Indirect competitors: solve the same pain through a different workflow.
- Adjacent players: could enter through data, distribution, or integrations.
- Potential acquirers: platforms that may buy or copy the capability.
- Non-consumption/current workaround: spreadsheets, email, manual work, contractors, internal tools.

## Customer discovery principles

Ask about past behavior, not future intention.

Avoid:

```text
Would you use this?
Would you pay for this?
Do you like this idea?
```

Prefer:

```text
Tell me about the last time this happened.
What triggered it?
Who was involved?
What did you do instead?
How much time or money did it cost?
What happens if this is not solved?
Where does this sit in your current workflow?
```

## Interview batch synthesis

After every five interviews, produce:

- Evidence that supports the hypothesis.
- Evidence that challenges the hypothesis.
- Surprising signals.
- Repeated language from users.
- Buyer/user differences.
- Segment differences.
- Questions for the next batch.

If supporting evidence is much longer than challenging evidence, ask whether this reflects the data or confirmation bias.
